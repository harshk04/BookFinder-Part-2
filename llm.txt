"To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee - This Pulitzer Prize-winning novel explores themes of justice, morality, and prejudice in a small Alabama town during the 1930s.
"1984" by George Orwell - This dystopian novel portrays a totalitarian society in which the government controls every aspect of its citizens' lives, from their thoughts to their actions.
"Pride and Prejudice" by Jane Austen - This classic novel of manners follows the relationship between Elizabeth Bennet and Mr. Darcy, two proud and prejudiced individuals in 19th-century England.
"The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald - This iconic novel explores the decadence and excess of the Jazz Age in New York City, as seen through the eyes of Jay Gatsby, a mysterious millionaire.
"One Hundred Years of Solitude" by Gabriel García Márquez - This magical realist novel chronicles the history of the Buendía family over several generations, set against the backdrop of Colombia's political and social upheavals.
"Beloved" by Toni Morrison - This novel explores the legacy of slavery and its impact on a family in the aftermath of the Civil War.
"The Catcher in the Rye" by J.D. Salinger - This coming-of-age novel follows the misadventures of Holden Caulfield, a disillusioned teenage boy struggling to find his place in the world.
"The Lord of the Rings" trilogy by J.R.R. Tolkien - This epic fantasy series follows the quest of hobbit Frodo Baggins to destroy the One Ring, which threatens to enslave Middle-earth.
"The Alchemist" by Paulo Coelho - This allegorical novel explores the themes of self-discovery, personal legend, and the interconnectedness of all things.
 "The Da Vinci Code" by Dan Brown - This thriller novel follows symbologist Robert Langdon as he uncovers a conspiracy involving the Catholic Church, the Holy Grail, and a secret society.
